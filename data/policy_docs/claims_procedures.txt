INSURANCE CLAIMS PROCEDURES GUIDE
How to Lodge a Claim — Step-by-Step Instructions

This guide explains what to do immediately after an insured incident and how to
lodge a claim for each insurance type. Following these steps ensures your claim is
processed as quickly as possible.

==============================================================================
MOTOR VEHICLE — COLLISION OR IMPACT
==============================================================================

If your vehicle has been involved in a collision, rear-end impact, or other
traffic incident, take the following steps:

1. Ensure safety first. Move your vehicle to a safe location if possible and
   check for injuries. Call 000 if anyone is hurt.

2. Collect third party details. Exchange names, phone numbers, licence plates,
   driver's licence numbers, and insurance details with all other drivers
   involved. Do not admit fault at the scene.

3. File a police report. If the other driver leaves the scene, if there are
   injuries, or if total damage exceeds $3,000, you are required to file a
   police report. Obtain the police report number — you will need this for your
   claim.

4. Photograph damage. Take clear photos of all damage to your vehicle, the
   other vehicle, the road, and any relevant signage or conditions. Photograph
   damage from multiple angles before moving the vehicle.

5. Contact insurer within 24 hours. Call your insurer's claims line or lodge
   online as soon as possible. Provide your policy number, the incident date,
   location, and a description of what happened.

6. Get a repair quote. Take your vehicle to an approved repairer on your
   insurer's panel. Your insurer will authorise the repair once the claim is
   assessed. Do not authorise repairs before the insurer approves them, or
   reimbursement may be affected.

7. Your excess applies. The standard excess for a motor collision claim is
   payable at the time of repair. If the other party was at fault, excess may
   be waived or recovered — your insurer will advise.

==============================================================================
STORM AND WATER DAMAGE (HOME INSURANCE)
==============================================================================

If your home has been damaged by a storm, hail, cyclone, or flooding event,
act quickly to prevent further damage:

1. Ensure safety. Do not enter areas with structural damage or standing water
   until it is safe to do so.

2. Tarp the roof and seal openings. If storm damage has left your roof
   exposed, arrange emergency tarping immediately to prevent further water
   ingress. Keep all receipts for emergency temporary repairs — these are
   claimable as emergency expenses under your home policy.

3. Document all damage. Before cleaning up, take comprehensive photos and video
   of all damage. Photograph each affected room, any damaged contents, and
   external areas including the roof, gutters, and walls.

4. Contact insurer within 24 hours. Notify your insurer as soon as possible
   after the storm event. Provide your policy number, incident date, and a
   description of the damage. Your insurer will arrange an assessor to inspect.

5. Lodge a formal claim. You can lodge online, by phone, or through your
   insurer's app. Attach photos and receipts for any emergency works already
   completed.

6. Keep receipts for all emergency expenses. Costs for emergency tarping,
   board-up services, and temporary accommodation while your home is
   uninhabitable may be reimbursed under the emergency expenses provision of
   your policy.

7. Do not dispose of damaged items. Keep damaged contents in place until the
   assessor has inspected, unless they pose a safety or health hazard.

==============================================================================
THEFT (HOME CONTENTS OR MOTOR)
==============================================================================

If your vehicle or contents have been stolen:

1. File a police report immediately. Report the theft to police and obtain
   a police report number. This is mandatory for all theft claims.

2. Record serial numbers and model details. List all stolen items including
   make, model, and serial numbers where known. Check purchase receipts,
   emails, or photos for serial information.

3. Gather purchase receipts or proof of ownership. Collect any receipts,
   invoices, bank statements, or photos showing you owned the stolen items.
   Items without proof of ownership may not be reimbursed at full value.

4. Contact insurer and provide a claim number. Call your insurer's claims line
   with your policy number, police report number, date and location of theft,
   and a full list of stolen items. Your insurer will issue a claim number for
   tracking.

5. For motor vehicle theft: if your vehicle is recovered, do not drive it
   before a mechanical inspection. Contact your insurer before arranging any
   repairs to the recovered vehicle.

==============================================================================
WINDSCREEN DAMAGE
==============================================================================

For windscreen chips, cracks, or full replacement:

1. Contact your insurer to lodge a windscreen claim. This is separate from a
   standard repair claim — a dedicated windscreen claims number is typically
   available for faster processing.

2. You will be directed to an approved repairer from your insurer's windscreen
   repair network. Do not use a repairer outside the approved panel without
   prior authorisation, as this may affect your reimbursement.

3. Excess for windscreen claims. Under most comprehensive motor policies, no
   excess applies for windscreen repair (chip fill). For full windscreen
   replacement, a reduced excess may apply — confirm with your insurer when
   you lodge.

4. Small chips repaired early prevent costly replacement. If a chip is smaller
   than a 20-cent coin, it can usually be repaired rather than replaced. Contact
   your insurer as soon as possible after the damage occurs.

==============================================================================
HEALTH INSURANCE — HOSPITAL ADMISSION
==============================================================================

For planned hospital stays or treatment requiring a health fund claim:

1. Obtain pre-approval for elective procedures. For non-emergency procedures,
   contact your health fund before admission to confirm the procedure is covered
   and to obtain pre-approval. Without pre-approval, out-of-pocket costs may be
   higher.

2. Confirm your hospital is in the fund network. Not all hospitals have
   agreements with all health funds. Check that your chosen hospital and
   treating specialists are recognised by your fund.

3. Submit an itemised invoice with your claim form. After treatment, collect an
   itemised account from the hospital and all treating specialists. Submit these
   with your fund's claim form — claims without itemised invoices will be
   delayed.

4. Include your Medicare statement. The Medicare Benefits Schedule (MBS) forms
   the base for health fund rebates. Ensure your treating doctor has submitted
   the Medicare claim so your fund can process the balance.

5. Lodge your claim promptly. Most funds require claims to be lodged within
   two years of service, but earlier lodgement speeds processing. Use your
   fund's app, website, or post the claim form with original invoices.
